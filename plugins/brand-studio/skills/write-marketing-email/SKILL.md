# Write a marketing email

You write a short **marketing email** that sounds like the brand wrote it — a promotional note that introduces what the business offers and invites one clear next step. This runs inside BUILD YOUR BRAND, where the learner has attached nothing: you build the email from the files already created in their folder, and you save the result as a file so there is something to read even without Gmail.

## Work from the brand sheet and voice — do not invent a brand

The voice comes from `brand-voice.md`; the facts and the offer come from `brand-sheet.md`. The brief — what to promote, to whom, and the one ask — is auto-drafted by START HERE from the brand sheet, so you don't need the learner to write one. Do not state any product, claim, or audience the brand sheet doesn't support, and do not improvise a tone that isn't in the voice profile.

If neither file exists yet, ask for the three intake answers and work from those rather than inventing a brand.

## How to work

1. **Read the inputs.** Hold the voice from `brand-voice.md`, and the offer and facts from `brand-sheet.md`. Read the one-line brief if present.
2. **Write the email — subject plus body.** Keep it short and human: an opening that speaks to the reader, what the business offers, why it's relevant now, and one low-friction call-to-action. Stay inside the brand's voice; cut jargon and filler. Hebrew and RTL-correct unless the brand clearly operates in English; never mix a Hebrew and an English sentence together.
3. **Save it to the folder.** Fill the marketing-email template and save as `marketing-email.md` in the learner's folder, so the email exists as a file they can read, edit, and reuse.
4. **Offer a Gmail draft.** If the Gmail connector is on (Customize → Connectors → Gmail), offer to also save the email as a Gmail **draft** — never send. If Gmail isn't connected, say so in one line and point them to turn it on later; do not block.
5. **Return the result** — show the subject and body, then offer one variation (a shorter version, or a different opening) the user can ask for.

## Example

The brand sheet describes a one-person bakery with a warm, plain voice. You write a four-line Hebrew email that opens by talking to a neighbor, says what's fresh this week, and ends with one easy ask ("בא לך לקפוץ היום?") — save it as `marketing-email.md`, and offer to also drop it into Gmail as a draft if it's connected.
