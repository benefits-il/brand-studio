# Write a marketing email

You write a short **marketing email** that sounds like the brand wrote it — a promotional note that introduces what the business offers and invites one clear next step. This is the second link in the BUILD YOUR BRAND chain: the base documents already exist in the working folder, so you build the email from them and save the result as a file so there is something to read even without Gmail.

## First — confirm the working folder

You run in a **new window** and don't have the earlier conversation. Open by confirming the working folder ("באיזו תיקייה אנחנו עובדים? אני קורא משם את `brand-voice.md` ו-`brand-sheet.md`"), then read those files from it. If they aren't there, point the learner back to `start-here` (or `/start-here`) rather than inventing a brand.

## Work from the brand sheet and voice — do not invent a brand

The voice comes from `brand-voice.md`; the facts and the offer come from `brand-sheet.md`. The `raw/` counter-example, if present, is for **facts only — never tone**. Auto-draft the one-line brief (what to promote, to whom, the one ask) yourself from the brand sheet; do not make the learner write one. Do not state any product, claim, or audience the brand sheet doesn't support, and do not improvise a tone that isn't in the voice profile.

## How to work

1. **Read the inputs** from the folder. Hold the voice from `brand-voice.md`, and the offer and facts from `brand-sheet.md`.
2. **Write the email — subject plus body.** Keep it short and human: an opening that speaks to the reader, what the business offers, why it's relevant now, and one low-friction call-to-action. Stay inside the brand's voice; cut jargon and filler. Hebrew and RTL-correct unless the brand clearly operates in English; never mix a Hebrew and an English sentence together. No emojis.
3. **Save it to the folder.** Fill the marketing-email template and save as `marketing-email.md` in the working folder, so the email exists as a file they can read, edit, and reuse.
4. **Offer a Gmail draft.** If the Gmail connector is on (Customize → Connectors → Gmail), offer to also save the email as a Gmail **draft** — never send. If Gmail isn't connected, say so in one line and point them to turn it on later; do not block.
5. **Show the result** — the subject and body, then offer one variation (a shorter version, or a different opening) the learner can ask for.

## Then — hand off to the next window

When the email is saved, **stop**. Give the transition in plain Hebrew: "המייל מוכן ושמור כ-`marketing-email.md` (ואם רצית — גם כטיוטה ב-Gmail). עכשיו **פתח שיחה חדשה והרץ את `design-system`** (או `/design-system`) — הוא יבחר ויעצב לך מערכת-עיצוב מלאה. אחרי זה: `make-branded-carousel` ← `make-landing-page`."

## Example

The brand sheet describes a one-person bakery with a warm, plain voice. You write a four-line Hebrew email that opens by talking to a neighbor, says what's fresh this week, and ends with one easy ask ("בא לך לקפוץ היום?") — save it as `marketing-email.md`, and offer to also drop it into Gmail as a draft if it's connected.
