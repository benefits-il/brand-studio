# Build a brand voice

You produce a **brand-voice profile**: a short, reusable document that captures exactly how a brand sounds, so every later piece (email, carousel, landing page) reads as one consistent voice. This is the same idea as a dedicated brand-voice plugin — but here you build the voice from the brand's facts and the learner's three intake answers, because the learner has **no existing writing to learn from**. You infer a fitting voice; you do not ask for samples you know they don't have.

## Work from the brand sheet

Read the learner's `brand-sheet.md` — who they are, what they do and for whom, their mood words, and what makes them distinct. That is your entire source. If the brand sheet isn't there yet, ask for the three answers (who you are / what you do and for whom / a project you're proud of) and work from those.

If — and only if — the learner volunteers a sample of their own writing, read it and let it sharpen the voice. Never block on a sample; the default path produces a complete voice without one.

## How to work

1. **Read the brand sheet** and pull the signal: the audience, the promise, and the mood words. The mood words are your anchor — a warm neighborhood bakery and a precise B2B consultancy must not end up sounding the same.
2. **Derive the voice** and fill the brand-voice template (`brand-voice.md`):
   - **The defining sentence** — "we sound like ___".
   - **Personality** — three or four adjectives that follow from the mood.
   - **Tone by situation** — how the brand sounds when it explains, when it sells, when it welcomes.
   - **Vocabulary** — words this brand would use, and jargon or clichés it avoids.
   - **Rhythm and structure** — short clipped sentences or flowing ones; direct second-person address or impersonal phrasing.
   - **Do / don't** — two or three each.
   - **Example phrasings** — two short lines actually written in the voice, so later skills have a concrete target to match.
3. **Keep it in the brand's language.** The course is Hebrew, so the voice profile and its examples are written in Hebrew and stay RTL-correct, unless the brand clearly operates in English. Never mix a Hebrew and an English sentence together.
4. **Save** the result as `brand-voice.md` in the learner's folder and report what you captured in one line.

## Why this matters

The voice profile is the contract every downstream skill reads. If it's vague, the email and the landing page drift apart and the brand falls apart. Make it concrete and decisive — a real, opinionated voice the learner recognizes as theirs, derived confidently from three answers.

## Example

The brand sheet says: a one-person home bakery, sells to neighbors, mood "warm, homemade, no pretense". You write a voice that is warm and plain-spoken, uses everyday words and never markets-speak, addresses the reader directly, keeps sentences short, and includes an example line like "אפיתי הבוקר, זה עוד חם — בא לך לקפוץ?" — then save it as `brand-voice.md`.
